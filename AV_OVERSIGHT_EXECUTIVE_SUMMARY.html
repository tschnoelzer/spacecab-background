Appendix: Source Studies

1. Building Trust Through Transparency: A New Federal Framework for AV Safety

Progressive Policy Institute | January 2026

Proposes unified US federal AV reporting standard. Key finding: AVs like Waymo show 91% fewer serious crashes than human drivers, with zero fatalities. Advocates two-layer approach: public summaries for transparency and detailed data for NHTSA review. Standardized reporting could facilitate AV integration in narrow lanes, boosting throughput by reducing errors.

2. Automated Vehicle Safety Framework

NHTSA | 2025 Update

Oversees AV testing via Standing General Orders, requiring crash reporting for ADS and Level 2 systems. 2025 amendments include domestic exemptions and voluntary data sharing via AV TEST tool. Capacity gains from shorter headways (up to 50% more vph in simulations). Emphasizes federal-state coordination to avoid patchwork regulations.

3. Safety Considerations for Automated Passenger Vehicles

Congressional Research Service | July 2025

Reviews NHTSA's role in FMVSS, investigations, and oversight. 2021 Standing General Order mandates crash reporting. AVs reduce human errors but require stakeholder input for deployment. Capacity: AVs enhance flow in mixed traffic, potentially increasing urban road efficiency by 20-40%. Highlights need for safety standards in narrow configurations.

4. Law No. (1) of 2026 Regulating Autonomous Vehicles in Ras Al Khaimah

UAE Government | January 2026

UAE emirate law assigns RAKTA oversight for AV operations, focusing on safety, cybersecurity, and data protection. Requires automatic safe-mode transitions, control center connectivity, and incident records. Enables structured AV deployment for smart mobility, reducing errors and congestion. Mandates monitoring and reporting for compliance.

5. Law No. (9) of 2023 Regulating the Operation of Autonomous Vehicles in Dubai

Dubai Legislation | 2023

Establishes RTA's framework for AV operations, aiming for AI-driven smart mobility and 25% AV trips by 2030. Covers licensing, inspections, and obligations; emphasizes safety standards and international best practices. AVs reduce fatalities by up to 90% and optimize traffic. Supports testing without safety drivers (e.g., Baidu RoboTaxis in 65 locations from 2026).

6. Dubai Self-Driving Transport Strategy & Roadmap

RTA | Updated 2023

Outlines roadmap for public/private AVs, targeting congestion reduction. AVs in shuttles/taxis boost capacity via connected systems; enablers include legislation and public acceptance. Capacity gains: 25-40% more efficient trips in urban networks. RTA manages certification and pilots. Supports narrow AV configurations for taxis/deliveries.

7. Impact of an Exclusive Narrow Automated Vehicle Lane on Adjacent Lane Driver Behavior

ScienceDirect | 2023

Simulator study on 9-ft AV lanes next to 12-ft human lanes. Key findings: No speed or mental effort changes; minor lane positioning shifts but no safety drop. Capacity: Narrow AV lanes double throughput in dedicated spaces without spillover. Recommends design for human-AV interactions. AV-enabled tiny vehicles in splits could enhance SZR flow.

8. Evidence AVs Can Operate in Lanes as Narrow as 9 Feet

SSTI | 2020 (with 2023 Updates)

Tests AV systems in narrow lanes. Consistent operation at 9 ft; safer for calming traffic. Capacity: Frees space for multimodal use, increasing overall efficiency. Suggests states adapt for AVs without widening. Supports half-lane AVs for congestion relief‚Äîdirectly applicable to Dubai's split-lane concept.

9. Impacts of Autonomous Vehicles on Freeway with Conditional Isolated and Dedicated Lanes

PMC | 2024

Simulation of AV lanes in mixed traffic. Dedicated lanes boost throughput at >70% penetration; 20-50% efficiency gains. Policies key for transitions. AV splits excel at 50%+ rates. For high-traffic scenarios like Dubai, dedicated AV lanes show substantial capacity improvements once AV penetration reaches critical mass.

10. Effects of Exclusive Lanes for Autonomous Vehicles on Urban Expressways

MDPI | 2023

Macro-simulation on Seoul expressways. AV lanes beneficial at 20-30% market penetration rate; capacity sensitivity higher at low MPR. Recommends introducing when penetration justifies. Aligns with Dubai taxi conversion for 20-40% gains. Seoul's experience provides validation for similar dense urban environments like Dubai.
